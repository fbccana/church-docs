    Standard Operating Procedure: Emergency Response¶

    Team: Safety Team
    Scope: Procedures for responding to immediate threats, medical emergencies, and natural disasters during church services and events.
    Objective: To ensure the safety, security, and well-being of all congregants, staff, and visitors.

    1. General Responsibilities¶

    All Safety Team members are expected to: - Maintain a calm and professional demeanor during any emergency. - Be familiar with the building layout, including all exits and shut-off valves. - Use designated communication channels (radios/phones) to coordinate responses. - Prioritize life safety over property protection.

    2. Medical Emergencies¶

    Goal: Provide immediate care and facilitate professional medical response.

    Immediate Actions¶

    1. Assessment: The first responder should assess the scene for safety and determine the nature of the emergency.
    2. Alert: Notify the Safety Team lead and designate someone specifically to call 911.
    3. 911 Call: Provide the dispatcher with:
      • Church address and specific location in the building.
      • Nature of the illness/injury.
      • Status of the victim (breathing, conscious, etc.).
    4. First Aid/CPR:
      • Retrieve the AED and First Aid Kit.
      • If the victim is unresponsive and not breathing, begin CPR and utilize the AED immediately.
      • Only provide aid within the scope of your training.

    Support Actions¶

    1. Crowd Control: Clear the immediate area to provide privacy and space for responders.
    2. EMS Access: Station a team member at the main entrance to guide paramedics to the victim's location.
    3. Documentation: Complete an Incident Report as soon as possible after the event.

    3. Fire and Smoke¶

    Goal: Rapid evacuation and accountability.

    Immediate Actions¶

    1. Alert: If fire or heavy smoke is detected, pull the nearest fire alarm and announce "FIRE!" over the radio.
    2. Evacuation: Instruct all persons to exit the building via the nearest safe exit. Do not use elevators.
    3. Zone Check: Ushers and Safety Team members should quickly check assigned zones (Restrooms, Classrooms, Nursery) to ensure no one is left behind.

    Support Actions¶

    1. Assembly: Direct everyone to the designated Rally Point (e.g., the far end of the parking lot).
    2. Accountability: Attempt to account for all staff and volunteers, particularly those in the Children's Wing.
    3. All Clear: Do not allow anyone to re-enter the building until the Fire Department provides an official "All Clear."

    4. Severe Weather (Tornado/High Winds)¶

    Goal: Protect individuals from flying debris and structural failure.

    Immediate Actions¶

    1. Monitoring: The Safety Team lead will monitor weather alerts during services.
    2. Notification: If a Tornado Warning is issued, the lead will coordinate with the Pastor or Worship Leader to make an announcement.
    3. Sheltering: Direct congregants to designated "Safe Zones" (interior rooms, lowest floor, away from windows and large-span roofs like the Sanctuary).

    Support Actions¶

    1. Protective Position: Instruct everyone to sit on the floor, cover their heads, and stay away from glass.
    2. Post-Storm: After the threat has passed, check for injuries and structural damage before allowing people to leave the shelter.

    5. Active Threat (Armed Intruder)¶

    Goal: Minimize loss of life through rapid response and neutralization of the threat.

    Protocol: Run, Hide, Fight¶

    1. Run (Evacuate): If there is a safe path, lead people out of the building immediately. Leave belongings behind.
    2. Hide (Lockdown): If evacuation is not possible:
      • Lock and barricade doors.
      • Turn off lights and silence all cell phones.
      • Stay out of the intruder's line of sight.
    3. Fight: As a last resort, and only when your life is in imminent danger, attempt to disrupt or incapacitate the intruder using physical force or improvised weapons.

    Communication¶

    • Call 911 as soon as it is safe. Provide a description of the intruder, their location, and the type of weapon.
    • When law enforcement arrives: Keep hands visible, follow all commands, and avoid making sudden movements.

    6. Post-Incident Procedures¶

    1. Debrief: The Safety Team and Church Leadership will meet to review the response and identify areas for improvement.
    2. Incident Report: A detailed written report must be filed for every emergency, including:
      • Date, time, and location.
      • Description of the event and actions taken.
      • Names of individuals involved and witnesses.
    3. Counseling: Ensure that staff and volunteers affected by the trauma have access to support and counseling.
