## Position Summary

To coordinate and plan the overall ministry to the Spanish-speaking people. The Pastor of Hispanic Ministry desires to reach Spanish-speaking people in the Corsicana area for Jesus Christ.

## Essential Functions and Responsibilities

### Hispanic Ministry Leadership (70% of time)

-   Lead a comprehensive ministry to Spanish-speaking people. This ministry will include worship service in Spanish, discipleship in Bible Fellowship and/or small groups, evangelism, pastoral ministry, and missions.
-   Develop leadership among our Spanish-speaking members.
-   Engage in personal visitation and evangelism.
-   Work alongside church staff, Mission Team, and ESL leadership to evangelize and disciple Spanish-speaking people who will become members of the FBC family.
-   Provide ministry and Bible study for Spanish-speaking employees at businesses that are receptive to this ministry (i.e., Casitas).

### Administration & Planning (30% of time)

-   Prepare and implement annual budgets for the Hispanic ministry.
-   Prepare and implement a calendar of activities which furthers the mission of the church focusing on the Hispanic population.
-   Meet with the Senior Pastor and church staff on a regular basis to coordinate Hispanic ministry.
-   Keep regular office hours at First Baptist Church.

## Qualifications

### Required

-   Has a clear call from God.
-   Has demonstrated a solid testimony of personal salvation through faith in Jesus Christ and moral uprightness.
-   Maintains a growing spiritual life with Christ through ongoing prayer, study of God's Word, and service to others.
-   Has served in a leadership capacity with a Hispanic church, either as Pastor or under the supervision of the pastor.
-   Has quality leadership skills.
-   Has the ability to listen and to communicate clearly, both orally and in written form.
-   Has effective speaking skills in both English and Spanish and the ability to speak confidently before groups.
-   Is competent using or willing to learn computer software.
-   Is in sufficient good health to discharge duties properly with or without reasonable accommodations.
-   Has effective administrative and organizational skills.
-   Has a Bachelor's degree and/or seminary degree and is licensed and ordained by a Baptist church.
-   Passes a criminal, financial, and driving background check as well as a drug screening.
-   Complies with all applicable federal and state laws.
