## Position Summary

Provides temporary reception and administrative support services when regular support staff members are unavailable, ensuring continuity of church office operations and maintaining professional first impressions for visitors and callers.

## Essential Functions and Responsibilities

### Primary Responsibilities (80-90% of time)

- Answer and direct all telephone calls and take accurate messages as needed
- Receive and assist all visitors to the church office in a professional and welcoming manner
- Handle incoming and outgoing mail including sorting, distribution, and mailing preparation
- Maintain church calendar awareness for directing inquiries and scheduling appointments

### Secondary Responsibilities (10-20% of time)

- Provide telephone or email notification of meetings and events as directed by supervisor
- Perform basic clerical duties such as filing, copying, and data entry as assigned
- Assist with other administrative tasks to support church office operations
- Other duties as assigned by Minister of Administration or regular office staff

## Qualifications

### Required

- Demonstrates personal salvation through faith in Jesus Christ with mature Christian testimony
- Professional phone etiquette and customer service skills
- Basic computer literacy
- Reliable and punctual with ability to work independently
- Professional appearance and conduct representing the church positively
- Alert judgment to know when to handle situations independently versus when to defer to supervisor

### Preferred

- Previous receptionist or office experience
- Familiarity with church operations and terminology
- Basic knowledge of office equipment (phones, copiers, computers)
- Flexible availability for varying work schedules

## Schedule & Work Environment

Temporary / As-needed basis; schedule varies by staffing needs and is assigned on an on-call basis.

Primarily sedentary office work with occasional lifting up to 25 pounds. Extended periods at computer workstation. Regular interaction with church members, visitors, and staff. Ability to maintain confidentiality of sensitive pastoral and membership information.
