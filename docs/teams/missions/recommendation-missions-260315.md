# Missions Committee Recommendations – March 15, 2026

## 1. Trauma Therapy Camp Sponsorship

**Project:** Bulgarian Trauma Ministry (Bulgarian Child Inc.)  
**Request:** Authorization to sponsor 20 Ukrainian children to attend trauma therapy camps in the summer of 2026.  
**Funding Source:** R.W. George Missions Fund  
**Total Estimated Cost:** $20,000 ($1,000 per camper)

### Background and Justification

The Missions Committee recommends utilizing funds from the R.W. George Missions Fund to provide critical support for children affected by the ongoing war in Ukraine. These children, aged 12 to 16, have endured four years of daily air-raid sirens, drone attacks, and the profound trauma of displacement and loss. Many are among the 5 million children forced to flee their homes, and most have lost their fathers to the conflict.

The sponsorship will fund 20 children to attend one of four 10-day trauma therapy camps held at "The Refuge" in the quiet mountains of Northwest Bulgaria between June 27 and August 21, 2026. This setting provides:

- **Safety and Stability:** A peaceful environment far from war zones and noise.
- **Expert Care:** Programs run in partnership with the Ukrainian Office of Veterans Affairs, the Ukrainian Association of War Wives and Widows, and the Chaplaincy of the Ukrainian Armed Forces.
- **Essential Rest:** An opportunity for young people from a waiting list of over 10,000 to find silence and healing.

The estimated cost of $985–$1,000 per camper covers all aspects of the therapy camp. Given that the mothers of these children typically earn less than $3 an hour, this life-changing opportunity is only possible through outside sponsorship.

**Recommendation:** The Missions Committee moves that the church authorize the use of $20,000 from the R.W. George Missions Fund to sponsor 20 children for the 2026 Bulgarian Trauma Ministry camps.

## 2. Trauma Therapy Camp Volunteer Subsidy

**Project:** Volunteer Support for 2026 Trauma Therapy Camps  
**Request:** Authorization to subsidize up to 18 volunteers at $2,500 each.  
**Funding Source:** R.W. George Missions Fund  
**Total Maximum Cost:** $45,000

### Background and Justification

The success of the trauma therapy camps at "The Refuge" depends on the support of 15 to 18 volunteers at each camp. These individuals will assist in restorative activities designed to rebuild the joy of childhood for Ukrainian youth, including:

- **Therapeutic Support:** Assisting in art, music, and group sessions.
- **Activity Leadership:** Sports, games, hiking, fishing, and swimming.
- **Skill Building:** Teaching hands-on skills (sewing, brick-laying) and conversational English.

### Funding and Commitment

The estimated total cost per volunteer is $3,000. To ensure the trip is accessible yet requires personal commitment, the Missions Committee proposes:

- **Church Subsidy:** $2,500 from the R.W. George Missions Fund (covering food, lodging, and travel).
- **Volunteer Contribution:** $500 per person, plus the cost of passports and personal expenses.

**Recommendation:** The Missions Committee moves that the church authorize a maximum expenditure of $45,000 from the R.W. George Missions Fund to subsidize up to 18 volunteers for the 2026 Bulgarian Trauma Ministry.

## 3. Good News Cupboard – Tuna Pouch Support

**Project:** Good News Cupboard (Internal Food Ministry)  
**Request:** Authorization to provide $1,000 to purchase a six-month supply of tuna pouches.  
**Funding Source:** R.W. George Missions Fund  
**Total Cost:** $1,000

### Background and Justification

The Good News Cupboard, an internal ministry of the church, provides essential food items to those in need within our community. To ensure a steady supply of protein for their distributions, the ministry is requesting funds specifically for the purchase of tuna pouches. These pouches are preferred for their ease of storage, long shelf life, and nutritional value.

**Recommendation:** The Missions Committee moves that the church authorize the expenditure of $1,000 from the R.W. George Missions Fund to support the Good News Cupboard's food distribution for the next six months.
