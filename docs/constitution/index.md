# Constitution

The Constitution establishes the foundational principles and governing framework of the church.

It defines the churchâ€™s **purpose**, **doctrinal commitments**, **membership**, and **organizational structure**. All other governing documents, including bylaws and policies, are subordinate to and must be consistent with the Constitution.

---

## Scope

The Constitution addresses matters such as:

- The mission and purpose of the church  
- Doctrinal beliefs and theological commitments  
- Church membership  
- Church governance and leadership structure  
- The relationship between the church and its officers

---

## Authority

The Constitution is the highest governing document of the church.  
Any amendments must be adopted in accordance with the procedures defined within the document itself.
