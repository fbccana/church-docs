    By-Laws Version 1.5¶

    BY-LAWS OF FIRST BAPTIST CHURCH OF CORSICANA, TEXAS1¶

    ARTICLE ONE. NAME AND PURPOSE¶

    The name and purpose of this organization shall be as set forth in the constitution adopted on May 5, 2004.

    ARTICLE TWO. MEMBERSHIP¶

    Section A. Admission¶

    Admission to membership in this body shall be upon majority vote of the members present and voting in any regularly scheduled meeting based upon the satisfaction of one of the following criteria:

    1. Upon profession of faith in the Lord Jesus Christ as Savior, and baptism by immersion in the name of the Father, the Son and the Holy Spirit;
    2. Upon transfer of letter from some other Baptist church of like faith and order;
    3. Upon restoration to membership, if, for various reasons, the name of an individual has been dropped from our roll;
    4. By personal statement of a Baptist, having been baptized by immersion into the fellowship of a Baptist church and whose church connection or church letter has been lost;
    5. By personal statement of an individual that he or she is already a Christian and was immersed by a church of like faith and order as set forth in #1 above.

    Section B. Watchcare¶

    Individuals may be received under the watchcare of the church for the purpose of worshipping regularly with our fellowship with no privilege of voting (designed primarily for non-Baptists).

    Section C. Termination¶

    Names of members may be removed from the roll in any of the following ways:

    1. Upon request from another Baptist church of like faith and order, the church may grant letters at its discretion;
    2. The name of a member will be removed upon the member's death;
    3. The name of a member who united with a church of another denomination may be removed from the roll by erasure when this church is notified by the member or by the church with which the member has united;
    4. A member may be dismissed from membership as a method of church discipline provided a three-fourths (3/4) majority of the members present and voting at a regularly scheduled business meeting of the church body in favor of withdrawal of fellowship.

    ARTICLE THREE. MEETINGS¶

    Section A¶

    The church shall hold regular meetings for the purpose of worship, prayer, teaching, training and fellowship on Sunday mornings, Sunday evenings, and Wednesday evenings. Other meetings of the church as a whole shall be set according to the needs of the church.

    Section B¶

    A church conference will be held on the third2 Sunday3 evening in each month (unless otherwise scheduled), at which time written reports will be received from the church clerk, The Finance Committee, and from the other standing committees or ministry teams as the church may require from time to time. When a report is to be made by a special committee appointed for a specific or limited purpose, prior notice of the intent to present the report will be given the membership by means of announcement in the church's regular publication. Any other business of the church may be considered at these monthly conferences without advance notice except as otherwise specified in the constitution or elsewhere in the by-laws.

    Section C¶

    A special church conference may be called by the Senior Pastor, the Chairman of the Deacons, or by majority vote of the members present and voting at a regularly scheduled worship meeting provided that written notice shall be given in the church's regular publication at least one week in advance of the special session stating the purpose of said meeting. A special church conference may also be called during any regular Sunday or Wednesday meeting by joint consent of the Pastor and the Chairman of the Deacons with the unanimous consent of the members present and voting.

    Section D¶

    All church conferences and meetings shall be conducted in accordance with standard parliamentary procedures as described in the latest edition of Robert's Rules of Order.

    Section E¶

    In the event of an emergency (fire, storm, natural disaster, etc.), the Pastor, and/or the Chairman of the Deacons is authorized to make decisions about the use of the church's property and/or resources to meet the particular emergency.

    ARTICLE FOUR. OFFICERS¶

    Section A. Administrative Officers¶

    The Senior Pastor, the Church Moderator, the Church Clerk, and the Church Treasurer shall constitute the Administrative Officers of the church. The term of service for the Church Clerk and the Church Treasurer will be for three (3) years.

    1. The Pastor shall hold the position of Church Moderator if he so desires. If the Pastor chooses not to be the Church Moderator, a Church Moderator will be elected annually by the church to serve for a period of one year, the term to begin on January 1st, following election. In the event the Moderator is absent from any regular or special church conference, the Chairman of the Deacons shall preside, or the members present and voting shall elect a temporary moderator to preside over that particular conference. The Moderator shall preside over the regular and special church conferences described in these by-laws and the constitution.

    Section B. CORPORATE OFFICERS¶

    1. The Corporate Officers of the church will consist of five members, which shall include a president, two vice presidents, a secretary, and a treasurer. The Corporate treasurer shall also be the church treasurer. The Corporate secretary will serve as the church clerk. The corporate officers shall be nominated by the Committee on Committees and elected by the church. Terms of service shall be three years with one or two officers rotating off each year as with standing committees. Those nominated and desiring to serve as corporate officers shall be active, participating members of First Baptist Church of Corsicana for a minimum of four years at the time the election is held. The date of membership entered into the church membership database will be used to establish length of church membership.
    2. The corporate officers shall be authorized to review policies and procedures, and to assess any potential risk that might arise from the day-to-day operations of the church with appropriate recommendations for action to mitigate the potential risk.
    3. The corporate officers will sign legal documents when necessary, and will consult with outside legal counsel when necessary to protect themselves and the church.
    4. The Corporate Officers shall initiate recommendations to the church, or receive recommendations from committees, regarding the usage or placement of funds other than those that are budgeted or designated.

    ARTICLE FIVE. DEACONS¶

    Section A. ELECTION¶

    The church shall elect from its membership, deacons in a number the church deems necessary to fill its needs. Deacons will be elected by the church in regular conference upon recommendation of the Deacon body. Those nominated and desiring to serve as deacons shall be active, participating members of First Baptist Church of Corsicana for a minimum of 2 years (if previously ordained, or 3 years for those yet to be ordained). The date of membership entered into the membership database will be used to establish length of church membership. The Deacon body shall elect its own officers including, but not limited to, a Chairman, a Vice-Chairman, and a Secretary

    Section B. DUTIES¶

    In accordance with the scriptural meaning of the word "deacon" and the example of the New Testament churches, deacons are to be leader-servants of the church. The following principles of service, based on Acts 6, define the purpose for deacons:

    1. To give attention to the needs of the membership, especially to widows.
    2. To assist the ministerial staff with secondary responsibilities in order that they may spend more time in prayer and in the ministry of the Word. (For example the visitation ministry, aid church committees in administration matters, ministry to the sick and needy, etc.) Recommendations from church committees and ministry teams will be presented to the Deacon body for consideration and then forwarded to the church for final acceptance or rejection.
    3. To assist in resolving problems which arise within the fellowship and work toward unity.
    4. To be supportive of the God-called leadership.
    5. To be examples within the fellowship of wisdom, spirituality, faithfulness and good character, and other examples in 1 Timothy 3:8-10.

    Section C. ACCOUNTABILITY¶

    REFER TO 1 Timothy 3:8-10

    With regards to attendance at deacon meetings, the following steps are designed to encourage the participation of the deacon body in monthly meetings and other deacon responsibilities.

    1. If unable to attend the monthly meetings, a deacon is responsible to contact an officer to inform him of his absence. That absence will be designated as an excused absence.
    2. If a deacon does not attend a monthly meeting and has not been given an excused absence, his absence will be considered an unexcused absence.
    3. In the event the deacon has three consecutive unexcused absences, the deacon will automatically be removed from the deacon roster.
    4. The deacon that has been removed from the deacon roster, remains an ordained deacon of the church.
    5. To restore a deacon's status on the deacon roster, he must request so in writing, and request an interview with the fellowship committee. There must be approval of reinstatement by a majority vote of the deacon body.

    ARTICLE SIX. STANDING COMMITTEES¶

    All standing committees shall be elected by the church in regular conference. Terms of service shall be three years, and approximately one-third (1/3) of the members of each committee shall be elected annually, in order that continuity be maintained in the work of the committees. A member who has served a three-year term on a committee is not eligible for election to any committee until at least one year has lapsed. A member of the Ministerial Staff will serve as advisor and ex-officio member for each standing committee as assigned by the Senior Pastor.

    Section A. COMMITTEE ON COMMITTEES¶

    The Committee on Committees (comprised of 9 members) shall be elected by the church in regular conference from nominations made by church members, prior to the conference in September. One-third of the committee shall be elected each year from nominations made by members. A ministerial staff member will serve as an advisor/ex-officio member. Those nominated and desiring to serve on the Committee on Committees shall be active, participating members of First Baptist Church of Corsicana for a minimum of four years at the time the election is held. The date of membership entered into the church membership database will be used to establish length of church membership.This committee will bring recommendations to the church for members of all standing committees except for the Committee on Committees. Service will begin in October of each year.

    Section B. FINANCE COMMITTEE¶

    Those nominated and desiring to serve on the Finance Committee shall be active, participating members of First Baptist Church of Corsicana for a minimum of two years at the time the election is held. The date of membership entered into the church membership database will be used to establish length of church membership. The Finance Committee will be comprised of nine (9) voting members. The financial secretary, the church treasurer, and a member of the ministerial staff shall serve as ex-officio members of the committee. The Finance Committee shall perform the following duties:

    1. Budget Expenditures: review financial expenditures each month, evaluate requests for special offerings by church organizations, and keep the church membership informed regarding receipts and expenditures.
    2. Monthly Reports: they shall report to the church at regular monthly church conferences on receipts and expenditures through the budget and any other authorized funds.
    3. Budget preparation: along with the *Budget Committee, they shall study expenditures and needs, estimate giving potential of the church, prepare an achievable budget for the coming year and present it to the church for its consideration.
    4. Shall review expenditures not included in the budget, relative to the availability of funds and potential impact on overall church finances, and make appropriate recommendations to the membership regarding temporary investment of excess funds.

    Section C. PROPERTIES COMMITTEE¶

    1. Those nominated and desiring to serve on the Properties Committee shall be active, participating members of First Baptist Church of Corsicana for a minimum of one (1) year at the time the election is held. The date of membership entered into the church membership database will be used to establish length of church membership.
    2. Membership of the Properties Committee is composed of six (6) members, and a ministerial staff person who serves as the advisor/ex-officio member.
    3. Properties Committee shall be entrusted with the responsibility of physical property, upkeep of current and future buildings and the operation of oversight of physical plant needs. This shall include buildings, furnishings, and permanently installed equipment and grounds.
    4. Properties Committee shall be responsible for oversight of the maintenance of all church properties, keeping them ready for use.

    Section D. PERSONNEL COMMITTEE¶

    1. Those nominated and desiring to serve on the Personnel Committee shall be active, participating members of First Baptist Church of Corsicana for a minimum of four (4) years at the time the election is held. The date of membership entered into the church membership database will be used to establish length of membership.
    2. Membership of the Personnel Committee is composed of six (6) members, and a ministerial staff person who serves as the advisor/ex-officio member.
    3. The Personnel Committee shall assist the Senior Pastor in matters related to personnel administration and management, including such areas as employment, salaries, benefits, personnel and services, and preparation of job descriptions.

    Section E. MISSIONS COMMITTEE¶

    1. Those nominated and desiring to serve on the Missions Committee shall be active, participating members of First Baptist Church of Corsicana for a minimum of one (1) year at the time the election is held. The date of membership entered into the church member database will be used to establish the length of membership.
    2. The Missions Committee, composed of six members, serving three (3) year terms, is charged with managing the monies that are designated for missions causes. The committee will work alongside a member of the ministerial staff to make recommendations to the church for the investment of these monies for mission trips and for mission work in Navarro County, in Texas, and around the world. Terms of service will begin in January.
    3. Each member of the Missions Committee must show personal conviction concerning mission work and a compassion for ministering to the hurting, and reaching the lost.
    4. The Missions Committee will bring recommendations before the church, concerning the use of undesignated monies for the support of missionaries on the field, mission causes around the world, and mission trips from First Baptist Church of Corsicana.
    5. The Missions Committee will work with the Finance Committee to provide financial support for mission work in the Corsicana area, in Texas, in the U.S., and around the world.
    6. The Chair of the Missions Committee will participate in the budget process for the support of mission causes through the annual budget.
    7. The Missions Committee shall bring recommendations concerning the goals for Mission offerings.
    8. The Missions Committee will assist and advise the Senior Pastoral staff concerning mission speakers and emphases that are designed to raise financial support.
    9. The Missions Committee does not have oversight of mission activities within the church. (i.e., Youth, Hispanic, etc.)
    10. The Missions Committee will meet quarterly or as needed during the year.

    Section F. TRANSPORTATION COMMITTEE¶

    The Transportation Committee consisting of three(3) members, and a ministerial staff member who will be an advisor/ex-officio member, shall be in direct control of all church-owned vehicles. They will formulate policies concerning the use of all church vehicles and update these policies when necessary. All policies shall be approved by the church.

    1. Those nominated and desiring to serve on the Transportation Committee shall be active, and participating members of First Baptist Church of Corsicana for a minimum of one (1) year at the time the election is held. The date of membership entered into the church membership database will be used to establish length of church membership.
    2. The Transportation Committee will ensure that all church vehicles receive regular and proper maintenance.
    3. The Transportation Committee shall recommend to the church for approval, all purchases and disposition of church vehicles.
    4. The Transportation Committee shall make recommendations to the Finance/Budget Committee, concerning its area of need and responsibility for the year.
    5. The Transportation Committee will meet as needed.

    Section G. OTHER SPECIAL COMMITTEES¶

    Committees may be elected to fill specific needs (other than Pulpit Supply and Pastor Search) and shall operate under the same general provisions as described in these articles for the committees specifically named. Such committees may be added, continued or discontinued as the church sees fit.

    1. Those nominated and desiring to serve on a Special Committee shall be active, participating members of First Baptist Church of Corsicana for a minimum of three (3) years at the time the election is held. The date of membership entered into the church membership database will be used to establish the length of church membership.

    ARTICLE SEVEN. PASTOR SUPPLY AND SEARCH COMMITTEES¶

    In the event the office of Senior Pastor shall become vacant, two special committees shall be elected: a Pulpit Supply Committee and a Pastor Search Committee.

    Section A. PULPIT SUPPLY COMMITTEE¶

    1. Selection: The Pulpit Supply Committee shall be nominated by the Committee on Committees and elected by the church. Those nominated and desiring to serve on the Pulpit Supply Committee shall be active, participating members of First Baptist Church of Corsicana for a minimum of four (4) years at the time the election is held. The date of membership entered into the church membership database will be used to establish length of church membership. The committee shall consist of seven (7) members.
    2. Duties: a. The Pulpit Supply Committee shall be responsible for securing an appropriate speaker for each worship service until more permanent arrangements can be made. b. The Pulpit Supply Committee shall, if appropriate, and desired by the church, recommend to the church for its consideration a person to serve as interim Pastor until such time as a Senior Pastor is called.

    Section B. SENIOR PASTOR SEARCH COMMITTEE¶

    1. Selection: a. The Senior Pastor Search Committee shall consist of seven (7) active members and one alternate who will attend meetings and who will be available to fill any vacancy which may occur on the Pastor Search Committee. b. The committee members will be selected under the supervision of the Personnel Committee as follows: c. Every church member shall have the opportunity to submit a single, written and signed list of nominations of not more than five persons for election to the Senior Pastor Search Committee. The Personnel Committee will, from those nominations, prepare a ballot of thirty nominees, observing the following criteria: (i) All nominees must be 21 years of age or older. Nominees must, in advance, give consent to serve if elected. (ii) Those nominated and desiring to serve on the Senior Pastor Search Committee shall be active, participating members of First Baptist Church of Corsicana, for a minimum of four (4) years at the time the election is held. The date of membership entered into the church database of membership will be used to establish length of church membership. (iii) Give primary consideration to the number of nominations each person received. (iv) No two members of the same family or related family shall be elected to the Senior Pastor Search Committee. The related man or woman receiving the lesser number of votes shall be dropped and the next highest man or woman shall be elected. (v) No church staff member or member of the household of a staff member shall be placed on the ballot. d. The ballot of thirty nominees will be published in the regular church publication and the election will be held at the Sunday morning service on the Sunday after the publication date. Each church member will have the opportunity to cast a ballot for the seven nominees. All ballots will be signed. The seven nominees receiving the highest number of votes shall constitute the Senior Pastor Search Committee. The person receiving the next highest number of votes will be the alternate.
    2. Duties: a. The Senior Pastor Search Committee, under the guidance of the Holy Spirit, shall consider and recommend to the church a person to be called to serve as Senior Pastor. The Senior Pastor Search Committee will work closely with the Finance Committee, the Personnel Committee, and other such committees as may be necessary. b. After its election, the Senior Pastor Search Committee will meet, select its own chairman, and organize itself to fulfill its duties. The Senior Pastor Search Committee shall make periodic reports to the church in conference on its progress. c. The work of the Senior Pastor Search Committee will be considered complete when a Senior Pastor has been called, has accepted and arrived on the field. The Senior Pastor Search Committee shall then be dissolved.

    ARTICLE EIGHT. SELECTION OF OTHER MINISTERIAL STAFF¶

    Section A¶

    The selection of other ministerial staff positions (Education, Music, Youth, Hispanic Minister, et. al.) shall be done through the service of the Personnel Committee and three members chosen from the church at large to form a search committee not to exceed a total of nine voting individuals (members). Nominations for the three additional members may be made from the floor of the church in conference. Those nominated must grant their permission to serve on a committee. The three receiving the highest number of votes shall be included on the Other Ministerial Staff Search Committee.

    Section B¶

    The Committee shall elect its own chairman and organize to fulfill its duties. They shall make periodic reports to the church in conference on its progress. The committee will work closely with the Finance Committee and other leadership in the areas of work (S.S., Music, Youth, Hispanic Ministry, etc.) as may be necessary.

    Section C¶

    This committee shall function until such time as they bring a recommendation to the church and the selection of a new staff member is made, after which they will be dissolved as a committee. The scope of the search of this committee will be confined to the specific position being filled.

    ARTICLE NINE. SELECTION OF MESSENGERS¶

    Section A¶

    Messengers to the annual meeting of the Baptist General Convention of Texas shall be elected by the church in accordance with the following procedure:

    1. Persons who are willing to be considered as messengers to any of these annual meetings shall inform the church office of their willingness to serve, at least thirty days prior to the scheduled meeting for which they are being considered as messengers.
    2. The names of volunteers shall be given to the Committee on Committees who shall, in the last regularly scheduled business meeting before the date that messengers must be appointed to be eligible to attend the applicable convention, present to the church its recommendations of persons to be elected as messengers and alternates.
    3. If full allotment of messengers has not been reached, additional nominations may be made from the floor if desired, and the church in conference will elect the appropriate number of messengers and alternates.

    ARTICLE TEN. AMENDMENTS¶

    Section A¶

    These By-Laws may be amended by the following procedure:

    1. Any proposed amendment shall be offered in writing at the regular church conference.
    2. The proposed amendment, together with the designated time for its consideration, shall be published in two consecutive issues of the church's official weekly publication thirty days prior to the meeting in which the vote is to be taken.
    3. The proposed amendment shall be approved by three fourths (3/4) of the members present and voting at each of two consecutive regular business meetings/church conferences after proper offering and notification described above.

    ADDENDUM¶

    Active and Participating member of First Baptist Church of Corsicana: A church member that is actively participating in the life of this local church. Several examples of activity in church life are:

    • Regular attendance at Sunday Services,
    • Regular attendance in Wednesday night activities, Bible studies, etc.,
    • Singing in the choir,
    • Attending or teaching in Sunday School,
    • Tithing,
    • Helping with Vacation Bible School,
    • Helping with security,
    • Working with Good News Clubs or Good News Caf,
    • Visitation program,
    • Helping with children's or youth programs on Wednesday nights

    ** This is not meant to be an all-inclusive list, but is a sample of the kind of activities that are there for active, participating members.

    Budget Committee: A joint committee comprised of the following:

    Finance Committee, Senior Pastor, Men's Ministry Coordinator, Women's Ministry Coordinator, Program Coordinator, Financial Secretary, Ministerial Staff, Church Treasurer, Chairpersons of Standing Committees.

    ** others may be included as needed when they have requests for budgeted monies.
